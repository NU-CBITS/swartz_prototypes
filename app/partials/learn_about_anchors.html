Learn About Anchors

As you have seen, The Social Rhythm Metric (SRM) tracks five activities or "anchors" that research has shown helps many people improve their mood.

Let's take a look at some examples of how anchors work in practice!

Click on the highlighted words below to learn more about how social rhythm anchors affect the lives of real people!

The Story of Jon

Jon gets up at 5:30 am. He sets the alarm on his phone for 6 am but he is usually awakened by his cat , demanding food. He goes to the kitchen, feeds the cat, and makes himself a cup of coffee . He then goes for a run, takes a quick shower , and grabs a breakfast bar on his way to the bus. He has to be at work by 8:30 am.

A Day in the Life of Maria

Maria is out of work . She tries, however, to set up meetings every day. Sometimes the meetings are to network for jobs. Other meetings are just to meet a friend for a walk or cup of coffee. Her finances are really tight, so she often skips lunch but she always tries to get some exercise , even if it is just a walk around the block. And, of course, she always calls her friend Anita to update her on her progress.

In Hal's Shoes...

Hal struggles with being a widower . The worst part is evenings. But he tries to keep things going , just like when his wife, Ann, was alive. He prepares himself a light supper at 5:30 pm , which is when they usually ate their evening meal. He watches the evening news at 6:30 pm . At 7 pm, he calls his son in Texas to say goodnight to his grandchildren. That gives him pleasure. He has trouble filling the time until 9:30 pm and often dozes off in front of the TV. At 9:30 pm, he takes his medications and gets into bed. He usually reads until he gets sleepy, and then he turns off the lights.
